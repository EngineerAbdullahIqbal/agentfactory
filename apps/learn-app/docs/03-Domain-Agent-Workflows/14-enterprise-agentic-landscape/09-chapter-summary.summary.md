### Core Concept

The chapter's frameworks are not independent models to be memorised separately. They form a connected decision system: each framework answers a specific question, and the answer to each question determines which framework you need next. The chain runs from problem diagnosis (Pilot Trap) through platform selection, monetisation framing, maturity assessment, and domain mapping to deployment conversation preparation. Three core insights tie everything together: the problem is knowledge transfer not technology, every decision flows from the knowledge question, and qualification must precede proposal.

### Key Mental Models

- **The Decision Chain**: Eight frameworks connected in sequence -- problem diagnosis, platform shift, knowledge worker centrality, platform selection, monetisation, maturity assessment, domain mapping, and conversation qualification. Each step depends on the one before it.
- **Knowledge Transfer as Root Cause**: Enterprise AI stalled not because the technology was insufficient but because there was no structural solution for transferring domain expertise into deployable agent instructions. The 2026 platforms solved this by putting knowledge workers in the authoring position.
- **Qualification-First Deployment**: The maturity model serves as a practical filter: determine what the organisation needs (education, direction, governance, optimisation, partnership) before proposing any solution.

### Critical Patterns

- The knowledge question (whose expertise, in what form, for whom, under what constraints) is the entry point for every deployment decision
- Platform choice follows from context: Cowork for team-level knowledge-worker-led deployment, Frontier for enterprise-wide executive-sponsored transformation
- Domain agents create value through four monetisation models determined by outcome measurability, work volume, and organisational embeddedness
- Most enterprises in 2026 sit at Levels 2 or 3; Part 3 deployments are designed for Level 2 entry and Level 3 full deployment
- The self-assessment checklist (eight questions) tests deployment readiness before moving to technical architecture

### Common Mistakes

- Treating the frameworks as academic models rather than practical decision tools for immediate use
- Believing you need to master every framework before taking any action -- start with the maturity assessment and knowledge question
- Seeing the chapter summary as a recap rather than a synthesis showing how frameworks connect as a system
- Skipping steps in the decision chain (e.g., selecting a platform before answering the knowledge question)
