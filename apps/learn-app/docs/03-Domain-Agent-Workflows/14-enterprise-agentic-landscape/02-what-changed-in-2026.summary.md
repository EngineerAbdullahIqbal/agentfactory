### Core Concept
The shift that arrived in early 2026 was architectural, not algorithmic. Production-grade platforms -- Anthropic Cowork and OpenAI Frontier -- put knowledge workers in the position of designing and deploying domain-specific agents, closing the knowledge transfer gap that caused the Pilot Trap described in Lesson 1.

### Key Mental Models
- **Platform shift**: The transition from developer-builds-expert-advises (2024) to expert-designs-platform-deploys (2026). The core insight is that domain experts are both the bottleneck and the solution.
- **Natural language configuration**: Domain experts describe agent behaviour in professional language, not code -- removing the developer bottleneck from agent design.
- **Structural positioning**: The professional who can encode and deploy domain expertise occupies a fundamentally different competitive position from the one who cannot.

### Critical Patterns
- The AI models of mid-2025 were already capable enough; the missing piece was the platform layer
- Information degrades at every developer-expert handoff; direct encoding eliminates that loss
- The February 2026 demonstrations crossed an accuracy threshold that disrupted enterprise procurement cycles

### Common Mistakes
- Assuming the 2026 shift was about better models (it was about better platforms for knowledge workers)
- Treating Cowork and Frontier as identical (they have different architectures, covered in Lesson 4)
- Thinking this shift only affects technology workers (it specifically targets non-technical domain experts)
