**Core Concept.** Most financial professionals do not build models from scratch — they inherit them. IDFA's five-phase retrofit process (Inspect, Identify Inputs, Order Dependencies, Rewrite Formulas, Validate) converts coordinate-based legacy models to IDFA compliance without changing business logic. The governing principle is transparency, not improvement: every output must match the pre-retrofit values exactly before any modifications are considered.

**Key Mental Models.** The retrofit is a conversion, not a rebuild — you extract and preserve the business decisions embedded in coordinates rather than reconstructing them from memory. Dependency ordering prevents the most common failure: rewriting a formula before its inputs have Named Ranges. The one-formula-at-a-time discipline isolates any validation failure to a single known change. Discrepancies during validation are either original model errors surfaced by the retrofit (discoveries) or inference errors introduced during conversion (mistakes), and the correct response differs for each.

**Critical Patterns and Common Mistakes.** The seven-step formula rewriting cycle (read, state business rule, write IDFA equivalent, LaTeX-verify if complex, confirm output, write to model, attach Intent Note) is the atomic unit of the retrofit. The most common mistake is attempting to fix business logic while retrofitting — this makes validation impossible because output differences could be intended improvements or accidental errors.
