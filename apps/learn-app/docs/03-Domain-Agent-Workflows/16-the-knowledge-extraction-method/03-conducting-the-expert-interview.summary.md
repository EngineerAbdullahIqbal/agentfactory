### Core Concept
The Method A interview is a structured conversation, not a form — and its success depends on three operational elements: a briefing protocol that establishes collaborative mode, a note-taking approach that captures specific rather than generic knowledge, and a north star summary written immediately after that anchors the first SKILL.md draft.

### Key Mental Models
- **Performance vs Collaborative Mode**: In performance mode, experts present polished, simplified accounts of their expertise. In collaborative mode, they think aloud about the messy, uncertain aspects — the pauses, qualifications, and self-corrections that contain the most valuable tacit knowledge. The briefing protocol establishes collaborative mode.
- **Generic vs Specific Notes**: "Checks DSCR" is generic and useless for a SKILL.md. "Checks DSCR first for term lending; below 1.25x flags for committee review; 1.25-1.5x requires covenant analysis; above 1.5x proceeds to sector exposure" is specific and directly translatable into SKILL.md instructions.
- **North Star Summary**: A one-paragraph distillation of the expert's core professional judgement — written immediately after the interview because the interviewer's sense of what mattered most fades quickly as conversational context recedes.

### Critical Patterns
- The briefing frames the interview as collaboration ("help us build an agent that works the way you would") not evaluation ("we are testing your expertise")
- Pauses and self-corrections during the interview often signal the richest extraction material — the expert is accessing tacit knowledge that resists easy articulation
- The north star summary becomes the anchor for the first SKILL.md draft and should capture the single most important thing the extraction must get right

### Common Mistakes
- Framing the interview as evaluation or dictation, which puts the expert in performance mode
- Recording generic notes that describe the role rather than the expert's specific operational logic
- Writing the north star summary the next day instead of immediately — a reconstruction rather than a capture
- Accepting the expert's first answer without follow-up probes that surface deeper knowledge
